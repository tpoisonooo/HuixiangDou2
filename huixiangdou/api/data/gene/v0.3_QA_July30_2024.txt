Q31. 解释水稻TAC4基因是什么？
TAC4 全名是Tiller Angle Control 4基因。
我们基于水稻日本晴（Oryza sativa ssp. japonica cv. Nipponbare）的第三代测序结果，并对语料进行深度检索发现，TAC4与SG2（small grain2）为同一基因，该基因与水稻分蘖角度调控紧密相关。TAC4基因位于2号染色体的正链上，基因起始位点至终止位点为15138546-15142385，序列全长3840bp，编码DUF659蛋白。
TAC4在不同数据库中的基因编号如下：水稻注释计划数据库(The Rice Annotation Project Database，RAP-DB)：Os02g0450000；水稻基因组注释项目数据库（Rice Genome Annotation Project，RGAP）：LOC_Os02g25230；稻属超级泛基因组数据库(Rice Super Pan-genome Information Resource Database, RiceSuperPIRdb)：AGIS_Os02g022060。

Q32: 水稻TAC4基因的亚细胞位置在哪里？
亚细胞位置（subcellular localization）是指细胞内特定蛋白质、核酸或其他生物分子在细胞内的具体位置或区域。这些位置包括但不限于细胞核、线粒体、内质网、高尔基体、细胞膜、细胞质、叶绿体、液泡等。确定分子的亚细胞位置对于理解其功能、相互作用及其在细胞中的作用机制至关重要。
基于截止2024年5月数据，我们发现TAC4编码的蛋白位于细胞核和细胞质中。

Q33. 预测水稻TAC4基因具有什么功能？

在RiceSuperPIRdb数据库中检索TAC4基因的T2T基因编号，TAC4基因的功能是一种表达蛋白，我们没有发现TAC4基因的GO分析和KEGG分析的结果。
在RAPDB数据库中搜索发现，TAC4基因有以下功能：类核糖核酸酶H蛋白；调控分蘖角度；通过油菜素类固醇信号途径调节籽粒的大小 (Os02t0450000-01)； 与OSIGBa0107A02.7蛋白相似(Os02t0450000-02)。 
在EnsemblPlants数据库中，我们没有发现TAC4基因的功能预测结果。

Q34.水稻TAC4基因有哪些功能已经被实验验证过?
截止2024年5月语料显示TAC4有2个功能，如下所示：
1.调节水稻分蘖角度。TAC4可通过增加吲哚乙酸含量和影响生长素分布来调节水稻地上部向地性，该调控路径与TAC1不同。TAC4突变降低了生长素合成基因YUCs和TAR2的表达水平，导致生长素含量下降，改变了生长素的分布格局，进而影响了植株地上部的向地性响应，最终影响了分蘖角度。
2.调控水稻粒型。SG2编码一个含有类核糖核酸酶H结构域的植物特异蛋白，该蛋白与BR信号通路的核心负调节因子GSK2互作并被GSK2磷酸化，且SG2的核质分布受BRs和GSK2调控。研究表明SG2作用于GSK2的下游正调控水稻粒型。此外，SG2与粒型负调控因子OsOFP19互作，在调控基因表达和水稻粒型方面部分拮抗OsOFP19的功能。

Q74 水稻OsWOX3A基因有哪些功能已经被实验验证过?

CHR709 plays a crucial role in the epigenetic regulation of rice development. Q119 What functions of the rice OsGLIP1 gene have been experimentally validated?
As of May 2024, the literature indicates that the OsGLIP1 gene has been experimentally validated for one function:
By regulating lipid metabolism, OsGLIP1 and OsGLIP2 exert negative control over disease resistance mechanisms in rice. These enzymes function as lipases capable of hydrolyzing lipids, thereby suppressing rice defense responses against bacterial and fungal pathogens. Conversely, downregulation of OsGLIP1 and OsGLIP2 enhances resistance of rice plants to Xanthomonas oryzae pv. oryzae and Magnaporthe oryzae. Conversely, overexpression of OsGLIP1 and OsGLIP2 significantly decreases disease resistance in plants. The increased accumulation of monogalactosyldiacylglycerol (MGDG) and digalactosyldiacylglycerol (DGDG) in OsGLIP1 and OsGLIP2 overexpressing plants likely contributes to this reduced resistance, akin to the effect observed with direct foliar application of MGDG and DGDG, which also diminishes resistance to bacterial blight. Furthermore, upregulation of OsGLIP1 and OsGLIP2 expression in transgenic plants that express OsGLIP1 protein lacking a signal peptide or with altered subcellular localization does not impair disease resistance, underscoring the critical role of OsGLIP protein subcellular localization in its immune function.

1. GDSL lipases modulate immunity through lipid homeostasis in rice

Q124: What functions of the rice SDR7-6 gene have been experimentally validated?

The SDR7-6 gene plays a pivotal role in rice resistance against bacterial leaf blight and blast diseases. EMS mutagenesis identified a light-dependent lesion mimic mutant, mic, which exhibits increased resistance to bacterial leaf blight and decreased susceptibility to blast disease. Genetic analysis of the lesion mimic phenotype determined it is governed by a single recessive gene. Subsequent positional cloning confirmed this gene as SDR7-6. Disruption of SDR7-6 induces cell death and adversely affects multiple agronomic traits.Both the mutant and SDR7-6 knockout lines demonstrated elevated levels of reactive oxygen species (H2O2), jasmonic acid, and antioxidant enzyme activities (SOD, APX, and POD), alongside heightened expression of pathogenesis-related genes. These results suggest that excessive ROS accumulation triggers enhanced peroxidase activity in the lesion mimic mutant. Furthermore, the SDR7-6 knockout line displayed reduced chlorophyll content and significant decreases in grain width, grain thickness, seed-setting rate, thousand-grain weight, single plant grain weight, panicle length, and plant height.

Q129: What functions of the rice OsHDA709 gene have been experimentally validated?
As of May 2024, the literature indicates that the OsHDA709 gene has been experimentally validated for one function:
The expression of HDAC genes in rice is tissue/organ-specific, with most HDAC genes responsive to drought or salt stress. Researchers classified them into three closely related groups: Group 1 includes HDA 705, HDA 702, HDA 709, HDA 710, and HDA 711; Group 2 comprises HDA 704 and HDA 713; Group 3 consists of HDT 701 and HDT 702. Different groups of genes exhibit highly similar expression patterns, showing relatively high levels in developing panicles and callus tissues. Expression analysis under abiotic stress revealed that HDA 709 and SRT 702 are upregulated by drought and suppressed by salt stress.
